Lab course as part of the M.Sc. Electrical Engineering program at KIT. The course introduces real-time simulation concepts and their application in energy technology, covering the full spectrum from plain real-time simulations to Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) testing.

Students gain hands-on experience with professional real-time simulation platforms and progress through increasingly complex HIL setups, with particular emphasis on Controller HIL (CHIL) and Power HIL (PHIL).

**Role:** Conceptualizing and running practical lab exercises, with emphasis on CHIL setups.

**Topics covered:**
- Real-time simulation concepts and theory
- Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) methodology for power engineers
- Plain real-time simulation of power systems
- Controller HIL (CHIL)
- Power HIL (PHIL)
- Practical experience with industrial real-time simulators (e.g. OPAL-RT)

**Tools:** OPAL-RT, Imperix, Egston, MATLAB/Simulink
