This community paper — co-authored by 21 researchers from leading European and international institutions — consolidates the collective experience of the Power Hardware-in-the-Loop (PHIL) research community and translates it into practical guidelines for industrial testing. The paper covers interface algorithms, stability criteria, hardware amplifier selection, real-time simulation requirements, and safety considerations. It bridges the gap between academic PHIL research and standardised industrial adoption, providing a reference framework for test engineers and system integrators.
