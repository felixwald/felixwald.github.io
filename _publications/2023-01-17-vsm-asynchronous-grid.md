This paper presents a Virtual Synchronous Machine (VSM) control strategy enabling grid-forming inverters to connect two asynchronous AC grids via a back-to-back power converter. The VSM mimics synchronous generator dynamics — providing inertia emulation, frequency droop, and damping — without requiring a phase-locked loop. The approach is validated experimentally through Controller Hardware-in-the-Loop (CHIL) and Power Hardware-in-the-Loop (PHIL) testing at the KIT Energy Lab 2.0.
