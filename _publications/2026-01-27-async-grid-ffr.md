This paper presents a system-level integration study of back-to-back (B2B) power converters connecting asynchronous AC grids, focusing on their capability to provide fast-frequency response (FFR) services. Using a VSM-based control architecture, the study evaluates how the B2B converter can detect and respond to frequency disturbances in either connected grid, injecting active power to arrest frequency deviations faster than conventional synchronous generators. Simulation results assess stability margins and the impact of control parameterisation on FFR performance in realistic grid scenarios.
