This paper investigates the potential of using grid frequency deviations as a control signal to coordinate distributed energy resources in distribution grids. By exploiting frequency-based power control, inverter-based resources and flexible loads can provide ancillary services that improve grid stability and support voltage and frequency regulation without requiring direct communication infrastructure.
